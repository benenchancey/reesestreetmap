Reese Street

The Reese Street neighborhood thrived in the mid-1920s as a cultural hub for Black Athenians, serving as a center of education, business, faith, and community despite ongoing segregation and limited opportunity.

Reese Street's Institutions

Reese Street was home to several educational institutions, including the Reese Street School, the Knox Institute, and Athens High and Industrial. Hill First Baptist Church also sits at the corner of North Pope and Reese. These community institutions were central to Reese Street's identity as a hub for Black life and education.
